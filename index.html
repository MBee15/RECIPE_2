A Taste of Home



Mubarak Umar
Good Food and a Warm Kitchen are What Makes a House a Home

I think back to my childhood and remember there wasn't a particular house i could call home. From the suburbs in Cincinnati, Ohio to towering penthouse apartments in the heart of Dubai, our family had cast our net of residence through all the corners of the globe. New York, Alexandria, Copanhagen, Milan... to name a few whimsical cities we lived in.

But now in hindsight, I realize home wasn't the grand chateaus or the presidential suites we lived in, but it was this dish my mother always seemed to make. Its rich velvety texture and its deliciously savory taste always to keep me grounded despite the long hours spent airborne during our travails. Having over 50 stamps on my passport before the age of 2 and now owning passports to over 30 different countries, I have ultimately come to realize that home is not a house but its a feeling.

That is also why I have grown to ignore the lies and falacies about my father being an international drug lord. Yes, I would admit that he does travel with large sums of cash and I have found enormous lumps of sugar neatly sealed in cellophane wraps in his room on numerous occasions. But that is only because of his aversions to the modern banking system and him having the occasional sweet tooth.

Nonetheless, this recipe is the embodiment of all those feelings of love and wonder from all those many years ago which was why i insisted my mother teach me the recipe as soon as I was able to hold a spoon. And now, I am incredibly excited to take you on this journey of flavor and umami, as I hope you feel even if it is a fraction of the wonder and joy I felt then. Today, I will be sharing my mother's recipe for sweet potato and yam pottage.

Let's Get Started

    First, we need to make our tomato mixture. Into a blender, add:
  • 15 medium sized tomatoes
  • 10 scotch bonnet peppers
  • A medium sized onion
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 5 cloves of ginger
      • Important Tips
  • Chop all the above listed ingredients and add a quarter cup of water into the blender to aid the blending process.
  • Blend into a thick, pasty consistency.
    • 20%
      Now for the aromatics. Into a small mortar and pestle, add:
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 5 cloves of ginger
    • Pound into a smooth consistency.
      • 40%




      Okay, Let's Get Cooking

        To a large pot on medium-high heat, add:
    • A half cup of oil
    • A chopped medium sized onion
    • The ginger-garlic paste
    • The tomato mixture
        • Important Tips
    • Ensure the oil is hot before adding the chopped onions.
    • Saute the onions for 1 minute before adding the ginger-garlic paste.
    • Cook for 2 minutes or until fragrant, then add the tomato mixture.
    • Cook for 10 minutes or until the mixture turns a rich deep red colour.
      • 60%

      The Meal is Almost Ready

        While our mixture simmers in the pot:
    • Peel a medium sized tuber of yam and 10 medium sized tomatoes
    • Cut finely into 2inch sized cubes, about an inch thick
      • 70%

      Back to Our Pot

        Reduce the heat to low and add:
    • A quarter cup of tomato paste
    • 2 tablespoons of curry
    • 1 sprig of fresh thyme
    • 1 sprig of fresh rosemary
    • 2 tablespoons of allspice
    • 4 small chicken bullion cubes
    • Salt to taste
    • Ground red pepper flakes to taste
      • Stir to evenly distribute the seasonings and aromatics
      • Bring the heat to medium and cook for 1 minute while stirring the pot
        • 80%




        Your Kitchen Must Smell so Good Now

          To our pot, add:
      • 6 cups of water
      • Our chopped yam and potato cubes
        • Cover the pot and bring the heat to high
        • Cook for 15 to 20 minutes or until the yam is soft
        • Ensure to stir every 4 to 5 minutes to prevent burning
          • 90%

          And We're Done!

          Dish and serve with any vegetable toppings you like

          100%
